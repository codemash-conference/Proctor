Session Proctor

Session Proctoring Checklist
For session proctoring, please show up for the session 10 minutes ahead of time so you can go through the "Before the session checklist.
You can pick up your time remaining cards at the registration booth.
For an room or A/V issues or adapters please call the Kalahari staff at 3-HELP.
Before the session
  • Thank the speaker for speaking at CodeMash
  • Verify the speaker can connect to the projectors.
  • Verify microphone works and can be heard in all parts of the room.
  • Ask the speaker if they would like any water
  • Ask the speaker if they need anything else.
  • Let the speaker know that you will be here the entire session and where you’re sitting
  • Let the speaker know that you will be holding up the time cards.
During the Session
  • Resolve any issues that may arise during the talk
  • Hold up the 20,10,5,0 time left cards at the appropriate times.
  • If the session is overcrowded, please call the registration booth so we can get more chairs or move the session to a bigger room.
  • Take start and end times and room counts.
After the session
  • Thank the speaker again.
  • Make sure the microphone(s) are left in the room
  • Make sure any other adapters are left with you and return to registration desk.

Sponsor Booth

Sponsor Arrival and Setup- Weds afternoon until about 8pm, and Thursday morning:
General
  1. Helping with all the "I need X" type requests. Usually the X is something like an extra power strip, chair, question about wall hanging, etc. In most cases, they will be telling the sponsor to check with the Kalahari and help get the right contact.
  2. Helping with all the "where are things at?" You'll help point out directions and locations to things. This is for both attendees and sponsors.
  3. Keeping a TODO list with contact info for any special requests while Jason and I aren't there.
  4. Dropping off sponsor sessions signs to booths
Scanning Related
  1. Sponsors will need to get their emails assigned so they can do scanning. The app is locked down a bit more this year, so I anticipate a lot of walk ups. The volunteer is responsible for pointing the sponsor to a google form where the sponsor can type in their email and sponsor company. After that, when I get back to the booth I will add them (and confirm they can scan).
  2. Help with the kiosk app (at the sponsor booth) so people can register their badge.
  3. We created a video this year to tell the sponsors how to do the scanning, so the volunteer will provide this information for people having issues/questions and general tech tips. Many sponsors don't really have much of a clue when it comes to technology.
Raffle Prizes (Friday)
  1. Managing the lunch raffle signup sheet (which should be easy this year- we're only doing it for Friday lunch).
  2. Giving info to sponsors on how the final prize raffle (email required info and a picture to cmraffle@gmail.com)
  3. Collecting raffle prizes at the sponsor booth. Later, helping organize them and load them up on the cart. Some prizes will go to KidzMash, others will be for CodeMash
  4. Helping schlepp the cart around to transfer prizes
